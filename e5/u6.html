 Bar graph/ Bar chart

/bɑː(r) ɡræf/, /bɑː(r) tʃɑːt/

(n.p) Biểu đồ cột


Bar graphs/ Bar charts are commonly used in presentations.

 Line graph/ Line chart

/laɪn ɡræf/, /laɪn tʃɑːt/

(n.p) Biểu đồ đường


The line graph/ line chart shows the information about the electronic items sold in 2015.

 represent

/ˌreprɪˈzent/

(v) Trình bày


The picture represents the main parts of a computer.

 Demonstrate

/ˈdemənstreɪt/

(v) Biểu thị


The pictures in the manual demonstrate how the machine works.

 Illustrate

/ˈɪləstreɪt/

(v) Minh họa


Here's an example to illustrate the revenue of Samsung company in 2015.

 Refer

/rɪˈfɜː(r)/

(v) Nhắc tới, đề cập tới


The technician refers to items which are suitable for the customer’s budget.

 Trend

/trend/

(n) Xu hướng


The graphs show a clear trend in the computer service sector.

 Percentage

/pəˈsentɪdʒ/

(n) Tỷ lệ phần trăm


A high percentage of the female staff are part-time workers.

 figure

/ˈfɪɡə(r)/

(n) Hình minh họa/con số


The measurement chart is full of figures, which makes me totally bored.

 Upward

/ˈʌpwəd/

(adj) Hướng lên


There was an upward trend in the price of refrigerator in 2010.

 Downward

/ˈdaʊnwəd/

(adj) Hướng xuống


There was an downward trend in unemployment rate last year.

 Rise=Increase

/raɪz/, /ɪnˈkriːs/

(n,v) Tăng


There has been a rise in the number of people out of work.

 Decline=decrease

/dɪˈklaɪn/, /dɪˈkriːs/

(n,v) Giảm, sụt


An increase in cars has resulted in the decline of public transport.

 Drop

/drɒp/

(n,v) sự hạ, giảm


We had to drop our prices because of the recession.

 Remain

/rɪˈmeɪn/

(v) Giữ nguyên, vẫn


Desktop computer prices are likely to remain unchanged.

 Remain steady

/rɪˈmeɪnˈstedi/

Ổn định


The oil price has remained steady over the last few years.

 Level off

/ˈlevl ɒf/

(v) Ngang bằng


Sales have levelled off after a period of rapid growth.

 Fluctuate

/ˈflʌktʃueɪt/

(v) Dao động


The price fluctuated between $20 and $40 an item

 Significant

/sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt/

(adj) Quan trọng, đáng kể


There are no significant differences between the two groups of students.

 Dramatic

/drəˈmætɪk/

(adj) Lớn, đột ngột


I need to see a dramatic improvement in your work.

 sudden

/ˈsʌdn/

(adj) Đột ngột


It was only decided yesterday. It's all been very sudden.

 Gradual

/ˈɡrædʒuəl/

(adj) Dần dần


The change is very gradual, it happens slowly over a long period.

 Steady

/ˈstedi/

(adj) Đều đặn, vững chắc


We are making slow but steady progress.

 Constant

/ˈkɒnstənt/

(adj) Không đổi


Prices have remained constant over this period.

 Peak

/pi:k/

(n,v) Đỉnh/ đạt tới đỉnh cao nhất


Traffic reaches its peak between 8 and 9 in the morning. Traffic peaks between 8 and 9 in the morning.

 Account for=make up

/əˈkaʊnt fɔːr/, /meɪk ʌp/

(n.p) Chiếm, gây ra


iOS accounts for 35% of smartphones’ operating system.

 Majority

/məˈdʒɒrəti/

(n) Phần lớn, đa số


The majority of people interviewed prefer TVs to radios.

 Minority

/maɪˈnɒrəti/

(n) Phần ít, thiểu số


Only a small minority of students is/are interested in history these days.
